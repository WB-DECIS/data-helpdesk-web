  • Visual Style:

    Body text should never stretch all of the way across a page on non-mobile devices. It is generally suggested that a Body Content component stretch across no more than seven Columns, even in the absence of a Left Navigation and/or right-rail content. Academic research strongly suggests that shorter line lengths have a substantial positive effect on both user engagement and comprehension.


    Responsiveness:

    Long-form Body Content should never occupy fewer than three columns in width. On small screens, Body Content should displace rail content if horizontal space is limited.


    Accessibility:

    Body content and the background colors must not fall below the WCAG recommended threshold ratio of 4.5:1, and optimaly should be above 7:1. More on accessibility standards can be found in the Practices section.
